this paper presents archaeological information about domestic dogs (canis lupus familiaris) recovered in prehistoric sites at the lowlands of uruguayan southeastern. the dog presence at the archaeological record is associated to the activity of horticultural hunter-gatherers groups, adapted to the very dynamic conditions of this flood ecosystems during the holocene. dog findings in mounds have recurrent and unique associations with burial areas. this circumstance allows starting the discussion of the economic, social and symbolic/ritual significance of these animals.
